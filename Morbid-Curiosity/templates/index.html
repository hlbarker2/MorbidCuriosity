Social Vulnerability Index

The Social vulnerability refers to the resilience of communities when confronted by external stresses on human health, stresses such as natural or human-caused disasters, or disease outbreaks. Reducing social vulnerability can decrease both human suffering and economic loss. The CDC's Social Vulnerability Index (SVI) uses 15 different U.S. census variables to compute the scoring.

What you see in these graphs is that when mortality data is compared to the SVI score, it is evident that the SVI is not a reliable indicator of health-related deaths in the United States.

Mortality Trends by Demographic Comparisons


There is not a huge difference in deaths based on the gender of the individual. One of the largest disparities is that men have a higher percentage of deaths through an accidents (7.4%) as compared to women (4.3%).
